Bilingual word games! Understand the intricacies of English-French translation. The purpose of this exercise is to better understand the relationship between the meaning of words in English and French. For each word game, you are given the following information

  • A word in English
  • A word in French
Your task is to answer the following question:

Does the meaning of the English word imply that of the French word? Yes or no.

For example:
  • Does "novel" imply "book"? Yes, because the English word "novel" is a special case of "book".
  • Does "poodle" imply "dog"? Yes, because the English word "poodle" refers to a breed of dogs.
  • Does "book" imply "novel"? No, because the English word "book" can be used to talk about books that are not novels.
  • Does "food" involve "cake"? No. The meaning of the word "food" is much more general than that of the French word "cake".
But the questions will not always be as easy ... You will often have to think about subtle nuances between French and English. Beware of false friends and misconceptions!
