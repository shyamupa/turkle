Bilingual word games!

Instructions

The purpose of this exercise is to better understand the relationship between the meaning of words in English and French. For each word game, you are given the following information

  • A word in English

  • A word in French
Your task is to answer the following question:

Does the meaning of the French word imply that of the English word? Yes or no.

For this task, a word A can imply B if any of the following holds true,

  • "A is a kind of B"

  • "A is a part of B"

  • "A causes B"

For example:
  • Does "roman" imply "book"? Yes, because the French word "roman" is a special case of the English word "book". (roman is a kind of book)

  • Does "caniche" imply "dog"? Yes, because the French word "caniche" refers to a particular breed of dogs. (caniche is a kind of dog)

  • Does "livre" imply "novel"? No, because the French word "livre" can be used to talk about books that are not novels.

  • Does "nourriture" imply "cake"? No. The meaning of the French word "nourriture" is much more general than that of the English word "cake".

But the questions will not always be as easy ... You will often have to think about subtle nuances between French and English. Beware of false friends and misconceptions!
